## 1. Executive Summary

Grade: B in every one of the eight dimensions (architecture, code-quality,
security, testing, performance, dependencies, devex-ops, docs). Up from the
prior audit's overall B, but not yet A anywhere. The quick-wins + M0 + M1
stack verifiably closed all four original High findings - the seal-index
lost-update race is locked, the 15-way repo-root fragmentation is unified
behind one census-enforced resolver, the production gate fails closed, and the
five untested live tool modules plus the MCP protocol envelope now have
behavior tests riding CI. That is real structural progress. What holds every
dimension at B is a consistent pattern: the A bar demands zero Critical/High,
Mediums either fixed or consciously accepted with a recorded rationale, and
health that is STRUCTURAL (enforced by a test/CI/one root primitive) rather
than aspirational - and there is a well-defined remainder that does not yet
meet it.

The one hard blocker that gates multiple dimensions at once: at this exact
HEAD the project is RED on all three windows-latest CI legs (ubuntu 3/3 green,
including the new py3.13 leg). The failures are entirely in fresh M1 trust
primitives - the locked_mutation lock breaks its own exactly-one-takeover
invariant and leaks PermissionError on Windows, an audit-append hammer hangs,
and the decision-ledger returns an os.sep-dependent path. A released,
cross-platform-promising tool cannot be graded A while its own release-critical
signal is red on a supported OS (this also violates the project's
init-platform-consistency hard rule).

Verification tally across the re-grade: of the named A-blockers and findings,
the adversarial pass CONFIRMED the large majority, ADJUSTED a handful
(severity re-calibrated - e.g. the scope-match '..' gap down to Low as a
finding, the resolver-census hole down to Medium), and REFUTED none
outright. The B grades are not soft.
